Quick read

When to choose each

Choose A for family energy

Best when Bank of Dad should feel like a Buddy Blocks sibling: kid-friendly, colorful, tactile, and easy to recognize on a phone.

Choose B for confidence

Best when Bill and Tara want the fastest path to a clean, durable, parent-first ledger that feels closest to a wallet app.

Choose C for personality

Best when the app should feel household-owned and memorable without leaning into either toy blocks or formal banking.

Option A

Coin Blocks

A buildable money world with coin tiles, chunky balances, sticker-like badges, and colorful account cards. Playful, optimistic, and kid-friendly without cloning Buddy Blocks.

Logo and Icon

Block-built piggy bank, coin tile, chunky BOD rhythm. Use full lockup on onboarding and the icon mark in the app shell.

Typography

Fredoka for display, Nunito Sans for UI, tabular numerals for every balance and transaction amount.

Components

Rounded tile cards, offset block shadows, bold action buttons, simple icon tiles, and friendly validation copy.

Option B

Vault Ledger

A compact private ledger with wallet, vault, and account-row cues. Trustworthy, restrained, clean, and fastest to turn into a durable app.

Logo and Icon

Wallet rectangle, vault dial, coin accent, and BOD monogram. App icon should feel credible beside wallet and password apps.

Typography

IBM Plex Sans for UI, IBM Plex Mono for balances and running totals, with tight numeric alignment.

Components

Flat bordered account rows, crisp forms, clear tables, subdued settings, and financial-app spacing.

Option C

Family General Store

A household counter and receipt-book direction. Every kid has a tab, every save or spend is a receipt entry, and the app feels personal rather than institutional.

Logo and Icon

Receipt tab, cash-drawer line, coin circle, and BOD counter label. Warm and household-owned.

Typography

Fraunces for headings, Atkinson Hyperlegible for UI, Space Mono for amounts and running totals.

Components

Ticket cards, receipt rows, stamped labels, practical buttons, and subtle ledger dividers.
